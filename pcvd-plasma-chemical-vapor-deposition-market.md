<p><p>Plasma Chemical Vapor Deposition (PCVD) is a technique used to deposit thin films of materials onto substrates through the chemical reaction of gaseous precursors in a plasma state. The market is segmented into RF (Radio Frequency) Plasma and DC (Direct Current) Plasma types. RF Plasma utilizes alternating current to generate plasma, offering uniform deposition over larger areas, while DC Plasma is typically more efficient for specific materials and faster deposition rates. Both methods are essential for various applications in electronics and coatings.</p></p>
