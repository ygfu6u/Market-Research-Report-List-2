<p><p>Superalloys used in turbocharger applications are categorized into three main types based on their primary alloying elements: iron-based (Fe-based), nickel-based (Ni-based), and cobalt-based (Co-based) superalloys. Fe-based superalloys offer good mechanical properties and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for low to moderate temperature applications. Ni-based superalloys excel in high-temperature strength and oxidation resistance, ideal for demanding turbocharger environments. Co-based superalloys provide excellent thermal stability and are suitable for extreme conditions, though they are typically more expensive, limiting their usage.</p></p>
